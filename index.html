About Us

Based on Census 2011 data, Dhakalgaon village in Madhya Pradesh, India, falls under the Barwaha tehsil of Khargone district. It is situated about 17km away from the sub-district headquarters of Barwaha and approximately 72km from the district headquarters of Khargone. Dhakalgaon also functions as a gram panchayat.

Administratively, Dhakalgaon is governed by a sarpanch, elected through local elections. In terms of political representation, the village falls under the Badwah assembly constituency and the Khandwa parliamentary constituency. For economic activities, the nearest town is Sanawad, situated roughly 7km away.

Our Popular Festivals

Gangour

Gangaur is a most special festival celebrated in our village. The Chaitra Gangaur festival begins with Gangaur Dashami or Ekadashi, during which the "baadi" or the area where barley is sown for the festival is prepared for eight days.
thereafter, songs are sung by women at baadi and the special dance performed is called jhalariya . Following this, villagers celebrate arrival of gangour mata for four days with supreme joy and happiness. and all wear new dress. women wear new saaris and jewellery.

Navratri

In our village dhakalgaon Navaratri festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm. The Garba event in our village is organized by Shri Raas Garba Mandal dhakalgaon, where different types of Garba, skits, inspirational dramas, and various presentations are held for 9 days. This is why our village's Garba Mandal is very famous. After Navaratri ends, on the tenth day, a huge competition is organized where Garba teams from nearby villages participate, and a competition is held among them.

Ramlila

The Ramlila of Dhakalgao village is extremely famous and it has been going on since the time of our ancestors nearly 150 years old. People from different villages come to watch this Ramlila. In the Ramlila, the villagers themselves perform, and people anthusiasticaly participate in it. The organization of this Ramlila is done by the Shri Balak Ramlila Committee. People from all the surrounding villages come to watch the Ramlila, and during this time, the atmosphere in the entire village becomes festive.
